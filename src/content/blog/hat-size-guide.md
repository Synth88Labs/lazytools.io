**A fitted hat size is just your head's circumference in inches divided by π — your head's diameter,
quoted in eighths of an inch.** A 57 cm head is 22.4 inches around; 22.4 ÷ 3.1416 = **7⅛**, a medium.
Measure once, and the [hat size converter](/size/hat-size-converter/) reads out US, UK and letter sizes
instantly.

## The fedora-era logic behind the eighths

Hat sizing predates flexible tape measures being standard shop kit. Hatmakers measured the wooden
head-forms ("blocks") hats were shaped on by their **diameter** — and a block for a 22-inch head has a
diameter of 22 ÷ π ≈ 7 inches. The eighth-inch steps survive from an era when a gentleman's hat was
fitted like a suit. So the strange-looking 7⅜ on a modern baseball cap is a 19th-century wood-block
measurement: it means "your head is about 7⅜ inches across."

## Measure your head in 30 seconds

1. Take a **soft measuring tape** (or string, then a ruler against it).
2. Wrap it **where a hat actually sits**: across the mid-forehead, just above the ears, around the
   widest point at the back of the skull.
3. **Comfortably snug** — you should slip one finger under the tape. Hair worn under the hat stays
   under the tape.
4. Read centimeters or inches into the [converter](/size/hat-size-converter/).

## Fitted vs adjustable vs stretch

- **Fitted** (dress hats, New Era 59FIFTY): exact eighths sizing — this is where the chart matters.
- **Adjustable** (snapbacks, dad caps): one size spans ~55–60 cm; the chart only matters at the extremes.
- **Stretch-fit** (S/M, L/XL flexfit): two-band sizing; if you're at a band's top edge, take the larger.

Material note: wool felt and leather sweatbands **shrink** modestly with sweat and rain — the classic
reason hatters say size up and pad with sizing tape rather than buy tight.

## Common hat-size mistakes

1. **Measuring across the forehead only** — the tape must round the widest back point or it reads small.
2. **Confusing US and UK fitted** — same-looking numbers, ⅛ apart; a UK 7 hat fits a US 7⅛ head.
3. **Trusting letters across brands** — one maker's L is another's XL; centimeters settle it.
4. **Buying tight "to break in"** — felt shrinks further; headache guaranteed.
5. **Ignoring hair changes** — thick hairstyles or a big cut change effective size by ~1 cm.

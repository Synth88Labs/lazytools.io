## The hidden cost of cloud productivity apps

A modern to-do app is astonishing value — until you notice what it knows. Your task list is a map of
your work, your worries and your plans; your calendar blocks reveal your habits; your notes hold
half-formed ideas you'd never publish. Cloud productivity apps store all of it on their
infrastructure, tied to an account, often analysed to "improve the product" and occasionally exposed
in a breach.

For genuine team collaboration, that centralisation earns its keep. But an enormous amount of
personal productivity — a focus timer, prioritising today's tasks, tracking a habit, timing a
workout — is solo, and doesn't need a server at all. Handing that data to the cloud is a cost with
no matching benefit.

## The local-first alternative

"Local-first" tools flip the model: the app runs in your browser and your data lives on **your**
device, in the browser's local storage. There's no sign-up, nothing to install, and nothing to
upload. The upsides are concrete:

- **Privacy by architecture.** Your data never leaves the device, so there's no server to be hacked
  and no account to be compromised. It's not a promise in a policy — it's how the tool is built.
- **Instant and offline.** No login, no loading spinners, no connection required once the page is
  open.
- **You own your data.** A one-click JSON export gives you a file you control; import it anywhere.
  No lock-in, no export-restricted "free tier."

## What you give up (be honest)

Local-first isn't free of trade-offs, and it's only the right choice when you know them:

- **No automatic sync.** Your data is on one device unless you manually export/import to another.
- **No real-time collaboration.** These are personal tools, not shared team workspaces.
- **Clearing browser data deletes the local copy.** This is exactly why the export button matters —
  treat a JSON export like a backup.

If you need a team wiki or a shared board with live cursors, use a cloud app; that's what it's for.
If you need to focus for 25 minutes, prioritise today's tasks, or tick off a habit, a local tool is
simpler, faster and private.

## Common questions, briefly

1. **"Isn't localStorage insecure?"** It's not encrypted, but it's local — the risk model is someone
   with physical access to your unlocked device, not a remote breach. For most personal planning
   that's fine; don't store secrets there (that's what the [file encryption](/security/file-encryption/)
   tool is for).
2. **"What if I clear my cookies?"** Local storage is cleared too — so export a JSON backup of
   anything you'd hate to lose.
3. **"Can I use these on my phone and laptop?"** Yes, but the data doesn't sync automatically —
   export from one and import to the other when you want them in step.

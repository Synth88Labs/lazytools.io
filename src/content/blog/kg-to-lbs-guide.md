**To convert kilograms to pounds, multiply by 2.2046 — one kilogram is exactly 2.2046226218 pounds.**
So 70 kg is 154.3 lbs, 80 kg is 176.4 lbs, and 100 kg is 220.5 lbs. For any other value, the
[kg to lbs converter](/units/kg-to-lbs/) computes the exact result instantly, in your browser, with the
working shown.

**Key takeaways**

- **Exact factor:** 1 kg = 2.2046226218 lb; 1 lb = 0.45359237 kg (defined by law in 1959, never changes)
- **Mental trick:** double the kg, add 10% — within 0.2% of exact
- **Airline anchor:** the 23 kg checked-bag limit = 50.7 lb, which is why US tickets say "50 lbs"
- **Gym anchor:** a 20 kg Olympic bar = 44.1 lb; a 100 kg lift = 220.5 lb ("two plates" in kg gyms)
- Down-rounding to "2.2" is fine for luggage, **not for medication doses**

## What is the exact kg to lbs formula?

**pounds = kilograms × 2.2046226218**

The number isn't a measurement — it's a legal definition. In the **1959 International Yard and Pound
Agreement**, the United States, United Kingdom, Canada, Australia, New Zealand and South Africa defined
one avoirdupois pound as exactly **0.45359237 kilograms**, ending decades of tiny national differences.
Dividing 1 by 0.45359237 gives 2.20462262185…, the factor every accurate converter uses. Because it is
a definition, kg↔lb is one of the few conversions with zero uncertainty, forever.

**Worked examples:**

- 55 kg × 2.2046 = **121.25 lbs**
- 70 kg × 2.2046 = **154.32 lbs**
- 90 kg × 2.2046 = **198.42 lbs**

## How do I convert kg to lbs in my head?

Use **double-plus-10%**:

1. Double the kilograms: 70 → 140
2. Add a tenth of that: 140 + 14 = **154 lbs** (exact: 154.32)

It works because 2 × 1.1 = 2.2, which is within 0.21% of the true 2.2046. Across everyday weights the
error never exceeds half a pound per 100 kg — accurate enough for luggage, gym plates and grocery math.
Where it is **not** enough: anything medical (see mistakes below).

Going the other way, **halve the pounds and subtract 10%**: 180 lbs → 90 − 9 = 81 kg (exact: 81.65 kg).

## Scenario: gym and weightlifting math

Barbell sport equipment is manufactured in kilograms worldwide — even in US gyms, competition plates
are metric. The conversions every lifter ends up doing:

| Equipment | Kg | Lbs |
|---|---|---|
| Olympic barbell | 20 kg | 44.1 lbs |
| Competition plate (red) | 25 kg | 55.1 lbs |
| Plate pair + bar ("one plate") | 60 kg | 132.3 lbs |
| "Two plates" | 100 kg | 220.5 lbs |
| "Three plates" | 140 kg | 308.6 lbs |

Meanwhile most US dumbbells and machines are labeled in pounds — so a lifter switching between a
45 lb American bar (20.4 kg) and a 20 kg Olympic bar is moving nearly identical weight, which is not a
coincidence: the 45 lb bar was designed as the imperial approximation of 20 kg.

## Scenario: airline baggage allowances

The standard international checked-bag limit is **23 kg because it converts to 50.7 lbs** — just over
the historic US 50-pound rule, letting airlines print both "23 kg" and "50 lbs" on the same fare class.
The premium allowance of **32 kg is 70.5 lbs** ("70 lbs"), which is also the maximum most airlines
accept before a bag must ship as cargo (many airport unions refuse single items above 32 kg for
handler-safety reasons).

Carry-on limits cluster around **7 kg (15.4 lbs)** in Asia and **10 kg (22 lbs)** in Europe. If your
home scale reads kilograms and your ticket says pounds — or vice versa — convert the exact number
rather than trusting the trick; airlines charge overweight fees on 0.5 kg excesses.

## Common mistakes when converting kg to lbs

1. **Using 2.2 where precision matters.** For a 70 kg patient, 2.2 vs 2.2046 differs by 0.32 lbs —
   irrelevant for luggage, potentially serious for weight-based drug dosing. Medical contexts use the
   exact factor or weigh in the target unit.
2. **Confusing stones with pounds.** UK body weight uses stones: 1 st = 14 lbs = 6.35 kg. "12 stone 8"
   is 176 lbs, not 128. There's a dedicated [kg to stone converter](/units/kg-to-stone/) for that.
3. **Dividing when you should multiply.** kg→lbs multiplies by 2.2046; lbs→kg multiplies by 0.4536
   (or divides by 2.2046). If your answer got *smaller* going kg→lbs, it went the wrong way.
4. **Rounding before the last step.** Convert first, round once at the end — rounding 2.2046 to 2.2
   *and* rounding the result compounds errors.

## Converting the other way: lbs to kg

The reverse formula is **kilograms = pounds × 0.45359237**. US product weights, dumbbells and shipping
specs land in metric paperwork constantly: 150 lbs is 68.04 kg; a 40 lb bag of dog food is 18.14 kg.

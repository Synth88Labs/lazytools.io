## What is the exact formula for kg to lbs?

The formula is:

**pounds = kilograms × 2.2046226218**

Where does that number come from? Since the 1959 International Yard and Pound Agreement, one pound is *defined* as exactly 0.45359237 kilograms. Dividing 1 by 0.45359237 gives 2.2046226218 — the factor every accurate converter uses. This is a legal definition, not a measurement, which is why kg-to-lbs is one of the few conversions that is exact and permanent.

**Example:** a 70 kg adult → 70 × 2.2046 = **154.32 lbs**.

## How do I convert kg to lbs in my head?

Use the **double-and-add-10%** trick:

1. Double the kilograms: 70 → 140
2. Add 10% of that: 140 + 14 = **154 lbs**

The exact answer is 154.32 lbs — the trick lands within 0.2%, which is close enough for gym plates, luggage and body weight. It works because 2 × 1.1 = 2.2 ≈ 2.2046.

## Why do gyms and airlines mix kg and lbs?

Equipment and regulations cross borders. Olympic weightlifting plates are manufactured in kilograms (a 20 kg bar, 25 kg plates), while most US gym dumbbells are in pounds. Airlines standardize checked luggage at 23 kg specifically because it converts to just over 50 lbs — a round number for US travelers. Knowing both units means never guessing at the rack or the check-in desk.

## FAQ

**Is 1 kg more than 1 lb?**
Yes — 1 kg is more than double: 2.2046 lbs. A kilogram is the heavier unit.

**How many kg is 150 lbs?**
150 × 0.45359 = 68.04 kg. Use the reverse [lbs to kg converter](/units/lbs-to-kg/) for other values.

**Is the 2.2 shortcut accurate enough for medication doses?**
No. Medical dosing should always use the exact factor (2.2046226218) or, better, measured weight in the required unit. The shortcut is for everyday estimates only.

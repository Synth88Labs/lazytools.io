**The catch with roofing is that the roof is bigger than the house it sits on.** Because it slopes, its
real surface area is larger than the ground footprint — and that's the number that decides how many
shingles you buy. Three steps get you from footprint to bundles.

<aside class="key-takeaways">

**Key takeaways**

- **Roof area = footprint × slope factor**, where slope factor = √(1 + (rise/run)²).
- **1 roofing square = 100 sq ft** (≈ 9.29 m²) — how roofing is quoted.
- **~3 bundles per square** for standard asphalt shingles.
- **Add 10–15%** for ridges, valleys, starter courses and waste.
- A **4/12 pitch** adds ~5% area; a **12/12 (45°)** adds ~41%.

</aside>

## Three steps

### 1. Footprint → roof area

Measure the **footprint** — the ground area under the roof — and multiply by the **slope factor**:

> roof area = footprint × √(1 + (rise/run)²)

The pitch is given as rise over a 12-unit run. A **4/12** roof rises 4 inches per 12 across, giving a
slope factor of √(1 + (4/12)²) ≈ **1.054** — so it's about 5% more area than the footprint. Steeper roofs
add much more: 12/12 (45°) is √2 ≈ **1.414**, a 41% increase.

### 2. Roof area → squares

Roofing is sold by the **square**, where **1 square = 100 sq ft** (≈ 9.29 m²). Divide your roof area by
100:

> squares = roof area (sq ft) ÷ 100

### 3. Squares → bundles

Standard asphalt shingles come about **3 bundles to a square**:

> bundles = squares × 3

Heavier or premium shingles may need 4–5 bundles per square, so check the wrapper. The
[roofing calculator](/home/roofing-calculator/) runs all three steps and adds your waste allowance.

## A worked example

A single roof plane over a **12 × 9 ft** footprint (108 sq ft) at a **4/12** pitch:

1. Roof area = 108 × 1.054 ≈ **113.8 sq ft**
2. Add 12% waste → ~127.5 sq ft → **1.3 squares**
3. Bundles = 1.3 × 3 ≈ **4 bundles**

For a whole house with two planes, do each plane and add them (or double a simple symmetric gable).

## How much to add for waste

Never order exactly the calculated amount:

- **Simple gable roof:** ~10%.
- **Hips and valleys:** up to 15% — those diagonal cuts waste more.
- **Plus** starter strips along the eaves and **ridge-cap** shingles along the top, which aren't in the
  field-shingle count.

## Quick summary

To estimate shingles, multiply the roof footprint by the slope factor √(1 + (rise/run)²) to get the true
roof area, divide by 100 sq ft for roofing squares, and multiply by ~3 bundles per square — then add
10–15% for ridges, valleys, starter and waste. Work it out with the
[roofing calculator](/home/roofing-calculator/), and see the other estimators in the
[Home & DIY tools](/home/).

**Every "how much do I need?" question is really one of two calculations.** If you're covering a *surface*
— paint, tiles, wallpaper — it's an **area** job. If you're filling a *depth* — concrete, mulch, soil —
it's a **volume** job. Get that straight and the rest is arithmetic (plus a sensible bit of waste).

<aside class="key-takeaways">

**Key takeaways**

- **Area jobs:** area = length × width. **Volume jobs:** volume = area × depth.
- **Paint** = paintable area × coats ÷ coverage (≈ 10–12 m²/L or 350–400 sq ft/gal).
- **Tiles** = area ÷ tile size, **+ ~10% waste** (15–20% for diagonal/herringbone).
- **Concrete & mulch** = volume ÷ bag yield → bags (round up); bulk beats bags past ~1 m³.
- **Always over-order a little** and buy from one batch for future repairs.

</aside>

## Paint (area)

Paint covers walls, so start with **wall area**: `2 × (length + width) × height`. Subtract the doors and
windows (a standard door is ~1.8–2 m²), multiply by the number of **coats** (two is standard), and divide
by the **coverage** printed on the tin:

> paint = (wall area − openings) × coats ÷ coverage

A 4 × 3 m room, 2.4 m high, two coats at 11 m²/L, needs about **5.4 litres**. The
[paint calculator](/home/paint-calculator/) does the subtraction and rounding for you — porous or bare
walls drink more, so use the lower coverage figure.

## Tiles and flooring (area)

Divide the **floor (or wall) area** by the **area of one tile**, then add waste for cuts and breakages:

> tiles = area ÷ tile area, then + 10% (straight) to 20% (diagonal/herringbone)

For 12 m² with 30 × 30 cm tiles: 12 ÷ 0.09 = 134 tiles, plus 10% ≈ **148**. The
[tile calculator](/home/tile-calculator/) also converts that to boxes. Buy everything from one batch —
tile shade varies between production runs.

## Concrete (volume)

Concrete fills a shape, so it's **length × width × thickness**:

> volume = L × W × thickness

A 3 × 2 m slab, 100 mm thick, is **0.6 m³**. Divide by a bag's yield (a 20 kg pre-mix bag makes about
0.01 m³) to get the bag count — around 65 with a little spare. Past roughly a cubic metre, **ready-mix
delivery is cheaper and easier** than mixing dozens of bags. The
[concrete calculator](/home/concrete-calculator/) adds ~7% for settling and spills, because running short
mid-pour creates a weak joint.

## Mulch and soil (volume)

Mulch and topsoil are spread to a **depth**, so it's **area × depth**:

> volume = area × depth

A 10 m² bed at 7.5 cm deep is **0.75 m³** (about a cubic yard). Keep mulch **5–8 cm (2–3 in)** deep to
suppress weeds without smothering plants. The [mulch & soil calculator](/home/mulch-soil-calculator/)
shows cubic metres, cubic yards and bags — and past about a cubic metre, bulk delivery wins on price.

## The golden rule: add a little

Every one of these should be **rounded up with a margin**:

- **Paint** — whole cans, plus a touch-up tin.
- **Tiles** — 10% waste minimum, more for patterns and cuts; keep spares for repairs.
- **Concrete** — 5–10% for settling and uneven ground.
- **Mulch/soil** — round up; it compacts.

And buy from the **same batch** where colour matters (paint, tiles, wallpaper), so a future repair blends
in.

## Quick summary

Ask "area or volume?" first. Area jobs use area = length × width — paint is area × coats ÷ coverage,
tiles are area ÷ tile size + waste. Volume jobs use volume = area × depth — concrete and mulch divide by a
bag yield for the bag count. Add 5–10% waste, round up to whole units, and buy from one batch. Run the
numbers with the [Home & DIY calculators](/home/).

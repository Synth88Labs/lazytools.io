# Next Non-STEM Category Vein — After the STEM Cluster & /home/ — 2026-07-11

Owner-directed strategic scan: with the STEM education cluster now deep and coherent
(`/math/` → `/statistics/` → `/biology/` → `/chemistry/` [31] → `/physics/` [29]), and a new
`/home/` (Home & DIY material-estimator) category being built **this session**, the question is:
**what is the single strongest non-STEM category to build AFTER `/home/`**, plus how far to
expand `/home/` itself.

Scored on the house x/25 rubric — **demand · competition-gap · durability ·
on-brand-differentiation · build-cost** (build-cost 5 = cheapest). `[S]` = declarative
fields→compute island; `[C]` = custom island (schedule / parser / chart / repeatable rows).
`[D!]` = data-staleness risk, `[D!-lite]` = trivially pinnable. Run in the same **owner-directed
ranked-expansion posture** as `/calc/`, `/biology/`, `/generate/`, `/web3/`, `/color/`,
`/chemistry/`, `/physics/` — competition-gap is a **structural 2–3 across every consumer vein**
(incumbents are already client-side, so "we don't upload" wins nothing), so gap is **not a veto**;
the edge is the proven house thesis: **exactness + working shown + clean single-purpose ad-free
SEO pages + honest sourcing + AI-resistance**.

---

## Executive summary

The single strongest next vein is a **dedicated `/finance/` category (Personal Finance &
Investing Math)** — the deterministic, staleness-proof money-math cluster that our existing
`/calc/` finance depth (compound interest, simple interest, mortgage + amortization, auto loan,
EMI, markup/margin, sales tax) already proves demand for, but which currently has no topical home
of its own. It **scores 20/25** and, uniquely among the candidates, has a **named, documented,
strengthening durability driver**: as of the 2026 Survey of the States, **39 states now require
personal-finance coursework to graduate** (26 standalone), on track to cover **~76% of the U.S.
Class of 2031** — a structural tailwind that grows demand for *exact, educational* finance-math
tools, and which happens to align perfectly with the compliance-mandated "educational, not
advice" framing that de-risks the YMYL exposure.

Every core finance tool is **deterministic and staleness-proof** because the user supplies the
rate — **there is no live-rate dependency anywhere** (compound/CAGR/ROI/rule-of-72/APR↔APY/debt
payoff are pure arithmetic). The flagship AI-resistant page is a **debt-payoff snowball/avalanche
calculator**: multi-debt month-by-month amortization is exactly the class of task chatbots botch,
and a user *should not* paste their full list of balances, rates and card names into a chatbot —
so it passes deterministic + privacy-sensitive + repeat-workflow + zero-friction AI-resistance
strongly.

**`/home/` expansion verdict: expand it robustly (build ~12–15 tools total, not 5).** It ties
finance at **20/25** on the rubric, is the **cheapest** category to build (pure geometry ×
user-supplied coverage rate), is **non-YMYL**, and its competition-gap is actually *slightly more
open* than finance (home estimators are fragmented, ad-choked and often just wrong). It is the
low-risk "keep shipping cheaply in parallel" category; finance is the higher-value **topical-
authority flagship** to build next. **Runner-up new category: Cooking & Kitchen** (broad recurring-
weekly-workflow demand, non-YMYL, mostly deterministic; one caveat is the ingredient-density
table). **Automotive is a close third**, carried almost entirely by two genuinely strong
deterministic tools (tire-size comparison and gear-ratio).

## #1 — `/finance/` (Personal Finance & Investing Math) — 20/25 — BUILD NEXT

- **demand 5** — investor.gov, Bankrate, NerdWallet, AARP, Ramsey, thecalculatorsite and dozens of
  standalone `*.debtcalculator.net`-class domains all monetize this heavily; our own shipped
  `/calc/` finance tools (mortgage, compound interest, EMI, auto loan) already prove the audience.
- **competition-gap 2** — structural: incumbents are already client-side and comprehensive. Edge is
  NOT privacy-of-upload; it is exact working shown + single-purpose ad-free pages + honest
  no-advice framing + the personal-data privacy angle (your debts/salary/net-worth never leave the
  device).
- **durability 5** — evergreen **and strengthening**: **39 states require personal-finance
  education to graduate (2026 Survey of the States; 26 standalone), heading to ~76% of the Class of
  2031** (NGPF / Forbes Apr-2026). This is a rare non-API structural driver that *grows* demand for
  exact educational money-math over 3+ years.
- **on-brand-differentiation 4** — the STEM-cluster house edge (exactness, steps, clean pages,
  cited sourcing) transfers cleanly; adds a genuine privacy wedge (financial figures are exactly
  what users shouldn't paste into a chatbot) and a strong AI-resistance story on the multi-debt /
  amortization tools.
- **build-cost 4** — ~70% are `[S]` field→compute; the heavy ones reuse the **already-shipped
  mortgage amortization `[C]` schedule + CSV component** and the compound-interest engine.

**YMYL flag: HIGH.** This is the reason to build it *carefully*, not to avoid it. Guardrails
(all mandatory, consistent with the spam-update / compliance memo):
1. **Educational, deterministic, sourced** — every page states the exact formula, shows working,
   and frames output as math ("here is what these numbers compute to"), never a recommendation.
2. **Persistent "not financial advice" note** on every page; **never** "are you on track / should
   you invest / is this a good return" language.
3. **User supplies every rate and assumption** — no live rates, no CPI series, no tax tables, no
   "typical return" defaults presented as guidance. This keeps it staleness-proof AND non-advisory.
4. Lead SEO with the *calculation* intent ("compound interest calculator", "debt payoff
   calculator"), not investor/decision intent.

### Build list (~13 tools, ranked)

**FLAGSHIP / LEAD (`[C]`, AI-resistant)**

1. **Debt payoff — snowball vs avalanche — 19/25 `[C]`** — `/finance/debt-payoff-calculator/`
   (+ satellites `debt-snowball-calculator`, `debt-avalanche-calculator`). Repeatable debt rows
   (balance / APR / min payment) + extra monthly amount; month-by-month multi-debt amortization,
   ordering by smallest-balance (snowball) vs highest-APR (avalanche), side-by-side payoff date +
   total interest + interest-saved delta; CSV schedule export. **AI-resistance 5/5**: deterministic
   multi-loop amortization (LLMs drift on the cross-debt rollover), binary-ish structured data,
   privacy-sensitive (a full debt list), repeat-workflow (bookmarked monthly). demand 5 · gap 2 ·
   dur 5 · diff 4 · cost 3.
2. **Compound interest **with regular contributions** — 19/25 `[C-lite]`** —
   `/finance/investment-calculator/` (+ `savings-calculator`). Fixes the real gap in the shipped
   `/calc/` compound-interest tool (it has no recurring-deposit term). FV of principal + periodic
   contribution (annuity), contribution timing (begin/end), growth chart + year-by-year table +
   CSV. The retirement/savings-goal workhorse. demand 5 · gap 2 · dur 5 · diff 4 · cost 4.

**SOLID (`[S]` unless noted — cheap, exact, high-leverage)**

3. **CAGR calculator — 19/25 `[S]`** — `/finance/cagr-calculator/`; `(end/begin)^(1/years) − 1`;
   FOLD annualized-return-from-ROI. Distinct exact intent vs simple ROI.
4. **ROI calculator — 18/25 `[S]`** — `/finance/roi-calculator/` (promotes the `/calc/` watchlist
   item). Net gain ÷ cost; annualized ROI row cross-links CAGR.
5. **Savings goal / how-long-to-save — 19/25 `[S]`** — `/finance/savings-goal-calculator/`
   (promotes `/calc/` watchlist). Solve for time / monthly deposit / required rate to hit a target.
6. **Rule of 72 (and 70/69.3) — 18/25 `[S]`** — `/finance/rule-of-72-calculator/`; doubling time,
   with the exact `ln2/ln(1+r)` cross-check shown. Cheap, viral, exam-relevant (education driver).
7. **APR ↔ APY converter — 18/25 `[S]`** — `/finance/apr-to-apy-calculator/` (promotes `/calc/`
   watchlist). `APY = (1+APR/n)^n − 1`; compounding-frequency aware; **deterministic, no live rate**.
8. **Loan payoff / extra-payment — 19/25 `[C]`** — `/finance/loan-payoff-calculator/` (promotes the
   `/calc/` opportunity; re-home). Amortization sim + extra-principal early-payoff; reuses the
   mortgage schedule component.
9. **Present value / future value / NPV (user-supplied cash flows) — 18/25 `[S/C]`** —
   `/finance/npv-calculator/`; discounted cash flow with a user discount rate; FOLD PV/FV single-sum.
10. **Break-even calculator — 19/25 `[S]`** — `/finance/break-even-calculator/` (promotes `/calc/`
    watchlist; small-business). Fixed ÷ (price − variable cost).
11. **Credit-card payoff / minimum-payment interest — 18/25 `[C]`** —
    `/finance/credit-card-payoff-calculator/`; months-to-zero at a fixed payment, total interest,
    min-payment trap illustration. Privacy + repeat-workflow AI-resistance.
12. **Down-payment / house-affordability (math only) — 18/25 `[S]`** —
    `/finance/down-payment-calculator/` (promotes `/calc/` watchlist). Keep to arithmetic
    (down-payment %, LTV); NO "how much house you can afford" advice framing.
13. **Real (inflation-adjusted) return — 17/25 `[S]`** — `/finance/inflation-adjusted-return-calculator/`;
    `(1+nominal)/(1+inflation) − 1` where **the user supplies the inflation rate**. This is the
    *deterministic slice* that is explicitly distinct from the rejected CPI-series inflation
    calculator (no dated CPI lookup).

### `/finance/` FOLD list (re-home / cross-link, do NOT rebuild)

- **Compound interest** (`/calc/`) → add a recurring-contribution term + APY row; canonical it into
  the `/finance/` hub (keep the `/calc/` slug as a cross-linked landing or 301 to `/finance/`).
- **Simple interest, mortgage + amortization, auto loan, EMI, markup/margin, sales tax,
  hourly-to-salary** (all `/calc/`) → surface under the `/finance/` hub via cross-links; the
  mortgage `[C]` amortization component is the shared engine for loan-payoff and credit-card payoff.
- **Savings goal / ROI / break-even / APR↔APY / down-payment / debt-to-income** — currently `/calc/`
  watchlist rows → build these **in `/finance/`**, discharging those watchlist items.
- **Retirement / 401k projection** (`/calc/` watchlist) → build as a mode of the investment
  calculator (#2) once its chart exists, NOT a separate assumption-heavy page.

### `/finance/` DO-NOT-BUILD (with reasons)

- **Currency converter** — live rates / CORS; hard charter exclusion (already rejected).
- **Live stock / crypto / market-index price, portfolio tracker, "should I buy" tools** — feed/CORS,
  rots instantly, financial-advice-adjacent (consistent with the `/web3/` live-price rejections).
- **Take-home / income-tax estimator** — jurisdiction tax tables rot (LLM-pricing-class maintenance
  liability); already rejected.
- **CPI inflation calculator** — needs a dated CPI series; already rejected. The user-supplied-rate
  real-return tool (#13) is the deterministic substitute.
- **Net-worth / budget / expense tracker with saved data** — needs persistence/accounts; off the
  static single-purpose brand (localStorage is possible but off-thesis).
- **Mortgage/loan rate-comparison or "best rate" tools** — require live rate feeds.
- **Any "on-track / advice / recommendation" framing** — safety exclusion; the whole category lives
  or dies on staying educational.

## #1= — `/home/` (Home & DIY) — how far to expand — 20/25 — EXPAND ROBUSTLY

**Verdict: build ~12–15 tools total, not the initial 5.** The category is the cheapest and lowest-
risk on the board, on-brand (deterministic geometry × user-supplied coverage rate =
staleness-proof), non-YMYL, and its competition-gap is *slightly more open* than finance because
home estimators are fragmented, ad-choked, watermark-free-but-ugly, and frequently produce
suspect numbers. The differentiation wedge is **honest waste-factor handling** (add a stated
%-overage), **multi-room / multi-coverage rows**, unit toggles, and a materials + rough-cost
breakdown — the things thin incumbents skip.

- demand 4 (seasonal but large: paint / flooring / concrete / roofing / drywall / gravel /
  mulch all high-volume) · gap 3 · dur 4 (evergreen DIY + housing; not a *strengthening* driver,
  which is why it supports rather than leads) · diff 4 · cost 5.

**Beyond the in-progress paint / tile-flooring / concrete / mulch-soil / wallpaper, expand with
(all `[S]` geometry × coverage):**

- **Drywall / sheetrock** (sheets + mud + tape + screws)
- **Roofing** (squares, shingle bundles, underlayment; pitch-aware area)
- **Gravel / aggregate / road base** (volume → tons via user-supplied density)
- **Topsoil & garden soil** and **sod / grass-seed** (area × depth / seeding rate)
- **Fence** (posts / rails / pickets / panels from run length + spacing)
- **Deck boards** (board count + fasteners from area + board width + gap)
- **Laminate / hardwood / vinyl flooring** with waste % (distinct from the tile estimator)
- **Tile grout & thinset**, **brick / block + mortar / bags**
- **Insulation** (batts/rolls to hit a coverage area; R-value as a labeled user input)
- **Stair stringer rise/run** and **ramp slope** (ADA 1:12 reference as a stated ratio)
- **Board-feet lumber** and **cost-per-square-foot / -yard** helper
- Optional borderline: **BTU room-sizing** for AC/heater (deterministic only if every factor is a
  labeled user input; otherwise defer — it drifts toward a lookup-table tool).

**`/home/` DO-NOT-BUILD:** anything needing **regional/curated reference data that rots** — R-value
recommendation tables by climate zone, local code/permit lookups, species-specific plant/hardiness
or fertilizer/pesticide-rate tables (same staleness reason the gardening/agronomy cluster was
rejected in the `/biology/` scan), material price databases, or "what does my project cost near me."
Keep every material factor a **user-supplied, overridable input**, never a maintained table.

## Runner-up new category — #2 Cooking & Kitchen (`/cooking/`) — 18/25

- demand 4 (huge, and **repeat-weekly-workflow** — the strongest AI-resistance axis: people bookmark
  a recipe scaler and use it every Sunday) · gap 2 (thecalculatorsite, CalcBE, CalcKitchen, King
  Arthur all client-side) · dur 4 · diff 4 · cost 4. **Low YMYL.**
- **Deterministic, on-brand tools:** recipe scaler (paste ingredients, resize by servings/multiplier,
  fraction-aware) `[C]`; baking-pan size/volume converter (round↔square↔rect, scale batter) `[S]`;
  baker's percentages / hydration `[S]`; cooking-time & doneness helper `[S]`; portion / servings.
- **One caveat (the reason it's #2 not #1):** cups↔grams **volume↔weight** conversion needs an
  ingredient-**density table**. It is a *stable* constant table (like the pinned CIAAW atomic
  weights), so it is `[D!-lite]` and acceptable if pinned/sourced — but it is a curation surface,
  and it's the single most-searched cooking intent, so the category can't fully dodge it.
- **FOLD:** oven-temperature °C↔°F↔Gas-Mark↔fan is a trivial deterministic converter — build it as a
  `/units/`-style page and cross-link, don't spin up density infrastructure for it.

## Third — Automotive (`/auto/`) — 17/25

- Carried by **two genuinely strong deterministic tools** the audience (our shipped `/calc/`
  auto-loan + fuel-cost) already implies: **tire-size comparison calculator** (section width /
  aspect / diameter → overall diameter, circumference, speedometer error, RPM/mi — pure geometry,
  big demand per tiresize.com) and **gear-ratio / axle-ratio** calculator. Both `[C-lite]`,
  deterministic, AI-resistant, non-YMYL.
- Thinner tail: engine displacement, compression ratio, 0–60 estimate, tire-pressure temp
  adjustment, fuel-economy trackers. The category is **narrow and fragmented** and several pieces
  overlap `/units/` and `/calc/`, which is why it's third — but **tire-size + gear-ratio are worth
  building even if the category isn't**, likely best as a small `/auto/` cluster anchored by those
  two plus the existing loan/fuel tools cross-linked in.

## #4 — Health & Fitness: DEEPEN inside `/calc/`, do NOT open a new category — 15/25

The best remaining items (one-rep max Epley/Brzycki, ovulation, sleep-cycle) are already **`/calc/`
watchlist rows**; pace/heart-rate was **repeatedly rejected** (McMillan/Strava/Omni-owned, no
driver). Health is **YMYL-HIGH** with weaker differentiation than finance and no strengthening
driver. Ship the one-rep-max + ovulation + sleep watchlist items **inside `/calc/`** as a health
sub-cluster; there is no coherent standalone category here.

## DO-NOT-BUILD as categories

- **Everyday / Lifestyle (12/25)** — a grab-bag with no coherence; its members already live in
  `/calc/` (tip, age), `/time/`, `/calendar/`. gap≈1. No topical spine.
- **Gaming / Hobby (11/25)** — dice probability overlaps `/statistics/` perm-comb; DPI / aspect-ratio
  overlap `/size/`; loot/gacha is off-brand novelty. Thin, no durable driver, off privacy-utility
  brand.

## Scan-lane notes

- **Demand:** finance (investor.gov / Bankrate / NerdWallet / debt-calculator domains), cooking
  (thecalculatorsite / CalcKitchen / CalcBE), automotive (tiresize.com / gear-ratio tools) all show
  dense, monetized, recurring incumbent supply → demand is proven; their ad-load and generic UX is
  our single-purpose opening. Same reality as every recent consumer scan.
- **Competition-gap:** structural 2–3 everywhere (client-side is baseline). Home is the one vein
  where gap edges to 3 (fragmented/poor incumbents).
- **Durability:** **finance is the only vein with a named, verifiable, strengthening driver** — state
  personal-finance graduation mandates (39 states, 2026; ~76% of Class of 2031). Others are evergreen
  but flat.
- **Regulatory / institutional:** the finance-education mandate is the institutional driver; no
  accessibility/e-invoice-class regulation applies to these consumer veins.
- **Feasibility / charter:** all recommended tools are pure client-side deterministic math with
  **user-supplied rates/factors** → no live feeds, no CORS, no rotting data. Every excluded tool
  fails on live-data, curated-data staleness, or the financial/medical-advice safety rule.

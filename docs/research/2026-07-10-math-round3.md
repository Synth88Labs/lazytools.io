# Browser-Tool Market Research — 2026-07-10 (Math round 3: competitor catalog sweep)

## Executive summary

Round 3 swept the math catalogs of Omni, calculator.net, CalculatorSoup, RapidTables, dCode and MathsIsFun, then scored the 12 fresh candidates from the brief. Every candidate exists at every major incumbent — the differentiating question is exact-arithmetic gaps, and three cleared the bar: an **exponent/power calculator** (22/25; BigInt all-digits + negative/fractional exponents as exact fractions/radicals), the **circle calculator "in terms of π"** (21/25; PROMOTED from watchlist — a fetch confirmed Omni's circumference calculator is decimal-only, and no major incumbent outputs symbolic π), and **Heron's-formula exact triangle area** (19/25 borderline; every incumbent is decimal, we can output simplified surds). Equally important: this round produced **seven fold recommendations** — factors/factor pairs, nth-root, ordering fractions, rounding, factorial, next-prime, and Pythagorean are all better served as enhancements to live tools than as new pages under the spam gate. Truth tables, set operations and interval notation are DO-NOT-BUILD: dedicated free domains own them.

## Opportunities

### 1. Exponent / power calculator — exact at any size — 22/25
- **Category/slug:** /math/exponent-calculator/
- **Score:** demand 5 · feasibility 5 · gap 3 · durability 4 · fit 5 = **22/25**
- **Future driver:** LLM arithmetic unreliability — a chatbot cannot reliably emit the 31 digits of 2^100; deterministic BigInt is the category's strongest AI-resistance case (established driver from round 1, still active). Secondary: ordinary calculators silently truncate past 15–16 digits (documented on GMAT/homework threads), a permanent hardware limitation.
- **Evidence (observed):** "Exponent Calculator" appears in ALL five swept catalogs (Omni, calculator.net, CalculatorSoup via exponential notation, RapidTables, dCode); calculator.net maintains BOTH an Exponent Calculator and a separate "Big Number Calculator" (two pages = proven distinct demand); MathsIsFun hosts a dedicated Full Precision Calculator; GMAT Club / Brainly threads ask for the exact digits of 2^100. https://www.calculator.net/exponent-calculator.html · https://www.mathsisfun.com/calculator-precision.html · https://gmatclub.com/forum/how-many-digits-2-100-has-17192.html · https://www.pearson.com/channels/calculators/exponent-calculator
- **Honest incumbent read:** Pearson's exponent calculator already does negative/fractional exponents with steps and exact toggle (they simply win on steps UX); MathsIsFun full-precision does all digits. Nobody combines *all digits + negative exponents as exact fractions (2⁻³ = 1/8) + fractional exponents as simplified radicals (8^(2/3) = 4, 54^(1/3) = 3∛2) + digit count* in one SEO-targeted page. Gap = 3, not 4.
- **Feasibility:** pure BigInt `a**b`; fractional exponents route through the existing radical/factorization engine; digit-count via decimal string length. No new algorithms.
- **MVP:** a^b with full digits + thousands separators + digit count; negative exponent → exact fraction; fractional exponent → exact simplified radical or integer when perfect; scientific-notation secondary line; laws-of-exponents FAQ (partially discharges the watchlisted exponent-rules item).
- **SEO/blog angle:** "exponent calculator", "2 to the 100th power exact value", "negative exponent as a fraction", "what is 8 to the 2/3 power". Blog: why your calculator lies after 15 digits.

### 2. Circle calculator — exact answers in terms of π — 21/25 (PROMOTED from watchlist, was 16)
- **Category/slug:** /math/circle-calculator/ (positioned as "in terms of π")
- **Score:** demand 3 · feasibility 5 · gap 4 · durability 4 · fit 5 = **21/25**
- **Future driver:** curricular permanence — "leave your answer in terms of π" is a standing instruction in US/UK geometry curricula, and LLMs botch exact-fraction × π arithmetic; steps-paywall trend (round-1 driver) still active.
- **New evidence that promotes it (observed):** fetched Omni's circumference calculator — decimal-only output (31.4159, 87.9646 cm; never 12π). calculator.net / CalculatorSoup circle calculators likewise decimal. piday.org's circle page *teaches* "12π cm, if writing in terms of π" but its calculator output is decimal/unclear — the intent is real enough that the #1 π-branded site explains it in prose, yet no major incumbent's calculator outputs it. https://www.omnicalculator.com/math/circumference (fetched) · https://www.piday.org/calculators/circle-calc-find-c-d-a-r/ (fetched) · https://www.calculator.net/circle-calculator.html
- **Honest read:** demand axis stays 3 — the homework phrase is real but we have no volume proof for the symbolic variant specifically; the head terms ("circle calculator", "circumference calculator") belong to Omni/calculator.net and we will not take them. This is a long-tail exact-intent bet.
- **Feasibility:** exact rationals × symbolic π token; radius given as integer/fraction/decimal → area = (r²)π as exact fraction·π with decimal secondary; solve any-from-any (r, d, C, A — A input in kπ form supported).
- **MVP:** circle (area, circumference, arc length, sector area) with exact kπ output + steps + decimal secondary; phase 2: sphere/cylinder/cone volumes in terms of π (fully discharges the watchlisted π-geometry item).
- **SEO/blog angle:** "area of a circle in terms of pi", "circumference in terms of pi calculator", "exact area of circle radius 6". Blog: why teachers want answers in terms of π.

### 3. Heron's formula / exact triangle area from three sides — 19/25 (borderline)
- **Category/slug:** /math/triangle-area/ (Heron's formula, exact)
- **Score:** demand 3 · feasibility 5 · gap 3 · durability 3 · fit 5 = **19/25**
- **Future driver:** same exact-arithmetic/LLM-unreliability driver; weaker than the two above (no new API or format, curriculum-persistent only).
- **Evidence (observed):** Heron's calculator present at Omni, MathsIsFun, MathOpenRef, Cuemath, BYJU'S, Vedantu — universal catalog presence proves demand; ALL output decimals. With integer (or rational) sides, 16·Area² = (a+b+c)(−a+b+c)(a−b+c)(a+b−c) is an exact integer, so Area is an exactly-simplifiable surd (sides 3,5,6 → 2√14) — no incumbent does this. https://www.omnicalculator.com/math/herons-formula · https://www.mathsisfun.com/geometry/herons-formula.html · https://www.mathopenref.com/heronsformula.html
- **Honest read:** the head term is saturated with steps-showing incumbents; our surd output is genuinely unique but the *exact-area* sub-intent is thinner than the π one. Ship third, or hold if the two above absorb the sprint.
- **Feasibility:** trivial — reuses radical simplifier for √N reduction; triangle-inequality validation; rational sides via LCD scaling.
- **MVP:** three sides → exact surd area + decimal + full Heron steps + semi-perimeter; degenerate-triangle detection; FAQ covers coordinate (shoelace) variant pointing to distance-midpoint.
- **SEO/blog angle:** "herons formula calculator", "exact area of a triangle with sides 3 5 6", "area of triangle without height".

## Fold recommendations (no new pages — spam-gate compliant, do these cheaply)

1. **Factors of a number / factor pairs → FOLD into /math/prime-factorizer/.** Would score 20/25 standalone but gap=2 blocks (CalculatorSoup, calculator.net, miniwebtool-with-steps, and calculat.io's programmatic "factors of N" pages own the long tail — a tail we cannot chase with generated pages under the spam gate). The live tool already computes divisor count and sum; **adding the actual divisor list + factor pairs is the natural completion** and adds "factors of X" / "factor pairs" keywords to an existing strong page. Highest-value fold of the round.
2. **Nth root / cube root simplifier → FOLD into /math/radical-simplifier/** as an index-n tab (∛54 = 3∛2 via prime-triple extraction). Standalone would cannibalize the live page (17/25). "Cube root of N in simplest radical form" queries are currently served by thin programmatic pages (numbermaniacs, online-calculator.org, Mathway popular-problems) — exactly the content class the June-2026 update pressures; one genuine tool tab beats them without page risk.
3. **Comparing / ordering fractions → FOLD into /math/fraction-calculator/** as a compare/order mode (LCD working shown, least-to-greatest). Standalone 19/25 but gap=2 (CalculatorSoup + a dozen clones already show LCD steps).
4. **Rounding to decimal places / nearest multiple → FOLD into /math/significant-figures/**, which already does digit-string rounding for sig figs. Standalone rounding calculator rejected: gap=2 (dedicated domain roundingcalculator.net, calculator.net, CalculatorSoup ×3, Omni's per-place programmatic pages) and no durability driver.
5. **Next / previous / nth prime → FOLD into /math/prime-factorizer/** ("nearest primes" row, dCode-style). Standalone prime-list page 19/25 but gap=2 (CalculatorSoup "1000 Prime Numbers" + CSV-exporting generators everywhere).
6. **Factorial (exact, all digits) → FOLD into /math/permutations-combinations/**, which already computes factorials internally; surface "n! =" with full BigInt digits + digit count. Catalog-sweep find (RapidTables/dCode both list it).
7. **Pythagorean theorem → FOLD a "right triangle / hypotenuse" tab into /math/distance-midpoint/** (legs a,b → c as simplified radical; solve-for-leg too). Standalone scores 20/25 but gap=2: Pearson's Pythagorean calculator already outputs simplified radicals (√45 → 3√5) with exact/decimal toggle and steps; Mathway/eMathHelp/calculator.net all show steps. The head term is the biggest in this round, so if the owner overrides the gap block (weighted-average precedent), build standalone /math/pythagorean-theorem/ reusing the distance engine — but the honest call is fold-first and watch Search Console.

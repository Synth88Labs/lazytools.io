# Browser-Tool Market Research — 2026-07-09 (Math round 2: converters & calculator depth)

Owner-directed second-round scan for /math/. Baseline: 16 tools live in /math/, percentage tools in /calc/, base converter in /dev/. Eleven fresh candidates evaluated; prior watchlist re-examined only where new evidence surfaced (none did).

## Executive summary

Round 2 confirms the round-1 pattern: demand is never the problem in math calculators — competition-gap decides everything, and the winning axis remains exact arithmetic + rules/steps shown free. Six candidates clear the bar, led by a **significant figures suite (22/25)** — two dedicated single-purpose domains prove demand, and the fetched CalculatorSoup incumbent shows no per-digit "why is this significant" reasoning — and a **degrees⇄radians exact-π converter (21/25)** where head incumbents (RapidTables, calculator.net-class) are decimal-first and nobody combines exact-π steps with DMS⇄decimal. Completing the square, synthetic/polynomial division, and a percentile-method-comparison calculator all score 20 on the strength of exact-fraction output where incumbents float. Weighted average has the highest raw demand of the batch (5/5) but is blocked at gap=2 — the grade-calculator space is saturated with free, adequate, steps-shown incumbents. The strict spam-gate call: percent⇄fraction⇄decimal three-way must NOT be built standalone (fold a percent line into the live decimal⇄fraction tool), and geometric/harmonic means fold into the live statistics calculator. A competitor signal worth logging: **Pearson's free calculator channel** surfaced in multiple unrelated queries this round (radians converter, exponent calculator) — a major education brand is systematically entering free-calculator SEO.

## Opportunities

### 1. Significant Figures Calculator & Counter — 22/25
- **Category/slug:** /math/significant-figures/
- **Scores:** demand 5 · feasibility 5 · gap 3 · durability 4 · fit 5
- **Future driver:** deterministic-verification in the AI era — sig-fig rules are exactly the kind of digit-level bookkeeping LLMs flub confidently; perennial chem/physics lab coursework (repeat weekly use per semester); steps-paywall trend at big solvers.
- **Evidence:** two dedicated single-purpose domains rank — sigfigscalculator.com and sigfigcalculators.com (strong standalone-demand proof); every major brand serves the term (calculatorsoup ×2 pages, omni, inchcalculator, gigacalculator, madformath). Fetched calculatorsoup rounding page: no indication it explains *which* digits are significant and why — "may lack detailed pedagogical features." URLs: https://www.sigfigscalculator.com/ · https://sigfigcalculators.com/ · https://www.calculatorsoup.com/calculators/math/significant-figures-rounding.php · https://www.omnicalculator.com/math/sig-fig
- **Gap:** incumbents count and round but rarely show rule-by-rule reasoning. Our angle: per-digit color-coded breakdown (leading zero → not significant, rule cited), count + round-to-N + sig-fig-aware arithmetic (add/sub → decimal places; mul/div → fewest sig figs) in one suite, scientific-notation output cross-linked to the live scientific-notation tool.
- **Feasibility:** pure string/digit logic, zero dependencies, fully deterministic including the trailing-zero ambiguity cases (surface the ambiguity explicitly — honesty differentiator).
- **MVP:** count sig figs with per-digit rule annotation; round to N sig figs; operation mode with the correct add-vs-multiply rule applied and named.
- **SEO angle:** "sig fig calculator", "how many significant figures does 0.00340 have", "rounding to 3 significant figures"; blog: the five sig-fig rules with the ambiguous-trailing-zero case handled honestly.

### 2. Degrees ⇄ Radians Converter (exact π forms) + DMS ⇄ Decimal Degrees — 21/25
- **Category/slug:** /math/degrees-radians/ (π-exact both directions; DMS⇄decimal as a second panel on the same page — do NOT split into a separate DMS page unless it earns it later)
- **Scores:** demand 5 · feasibility 5 · gap 3 · durability 3 · fit 5
- **Future driver:** perennial trig/precalc coursework; deterministic exact-form verification (homework requires π/4, not 0.7854 — a form LLMs and most converters don't reliably produce simplified).
- **Evidence:** dedicated domain radianstodegrees.net exists (demand proof); head incumbents rapidtables/inchcalculator/calculator-academy serve decimal-first (fetched RapidTables: calculator outputs decimal; π forms only in the static lookup table; no DMS in the converter itself, separate pages). Minor sites (clickcalculators.com) and Pearson's new channel do π-exact steps — the intent is real and only weakly served by majors. URLs: https://www.rapidtables.com/convert/number/degrees-to-radians.html · https://clickcalculators.com/degree-to-radian-converter/ · https://www.pearson.com/channels/calculators/radians-degrees-converter · https://www.radianstodegrees.net/degrees-to-radians
- **Gap:** π-exact simplification (137° = 137π/180, 45° = π/4) with the reduce-the-fraction step shown, both directions (radians input accepts "5π/6"), plus DMS⇄decimal with the ×60 working — no major incumbent combines these. Reuses the live rational/GCD engine wholesale.
- **Feasibility:** exact rational arithmetic on (deg/180); DMS is exact base-60; decimal shown as secondary line.
- **MVP:** deg→rad exact-π + decimal, rad→deg (accepts kπ/n and decimals), DMS⇄decimal degrees, unit-circle common-angles table.
- **SEO angle:** "degrees to radians in terms of pi", "45 degrees in radians", "convert dms to decimal degrees"; blog: the unit circle and why exact π forms matter.

### 3. Completing the Square / Vertex Form Converter — 20/25
- **Category/slug:** /math/completing-the-square/ (owns both "completing the square calculator" and "vertex form calculator" intents on ONE page — spam gate: no separate vertex-form page)
- **Scores:** demand 4 · feasibility 5 · gap 3 · durability 3 · fit 5
- **Future driver:** steps-paywall (Symbolab gates full CTS steps behind Pro); exact-fraction vertex output is rare; algebra coursework perennial.
- **Evidence:** crowded head term — analyzemath, calculatorsoup, symbolab, mathcracker, cuemath, vedantu all serve it — but the free-steps incumbents compute decimally for a≠1 cases. URLs: https://www.analyzemath.com/Calculators/complete_the_square_calculator.html · https://www.calculatorsoup.com/calculators/algebra/completing-the-square-calculator.php · https://www.symbolab.com/solver/complete-the-square-calculator · https://mathcracker.com/vertex-form-calculator
- **Gap:** exact rational h = −b/2a, k = c − b²/4a as simplified fractions with every step (factor out a, half-the-coefficient, square it) — e.g. 3x²+5x−1 → 3(x+5/6)² − 37/12 exact. Cannibalization check: live quadratic solver is root-focused (radical roots + factored form) and does NOT emit vertex form — distinct query intent, cross-link both ways.
- **Feasibility:** direct reuse of the live quadratic Fraction engine; steps renderer already exists.
- **MVP:** ax²+bx+c → vertex form with full worked steps, vertex + axis of symmetry, optional solve-from-CTS (roots derived by square-rooting both sides, tying back to the quadratic formula).
- **SEO angle:** "completing the square calculator with steps", "vertex form converter", "convert to vertex form fraction"; blog: where the quadratic formula comes from (derived by CTS).

### 4. Synthetic Division + Polynomial Long Division — 20/25
- **Category/slug:** /math/synthetic-division/ (synthetic tableau + polynomial long division as two tabs on one page; two intents but shared engine — revisit a split only with traffic proof)
- **Scores:** demand 4 · feasibility 5 · gap 3 · durability 3 · fit 5
- **Future driver:** steps-paywall (Symbolab); LLMs notoriously botch the bring-down arithmetic in synthetic tableaus — deterministic verification driver; Algebra 2/precalc perennial.
- **Evidence:** served by emathhelp, symbolab, mathportal (free full steps — the strongest incumbent), mathcracker, snapxam, plus ad-heavy calculator-online.net/allmath. URLs: https://www.mathportal.org/calculators/polynomials-solvers/synthetic-division-calculator.php · https://www.emathhelp.net/calculators/algebra-1/synthetic-division-calculator/ · https://www.symbolab.com/solver/synthetic-division-calculator · https://www.emathhelp.net/calculators/algebra-1/polynomial-long-division-calculator/
- **Gap:** moderate (mathportal is genuinely good) — our edge: exact rational coefficients throughout (divide by x − 2/3 without decimals), both methods side by side with the "why synthetic only works for linear divisors" explanation, remainder-theorem readout (P(c) = remainder) and is-it-a-factor verdict. Pairs directly with the shipped long-division success and the binomial-expansion audience.
- **Feasibility:** array-of-Fractions arithmetic; tableau renders like the live long-division layout.
- **MVP:** synthetic division by (x − c) with c rational, full tableau; polynomial long division for any divisor degree with worked layout; remainder theorem + factor check callouts.
- **SEO angle:** "synthetic division calculator with steps", "polynomial long division calculator", "remainder theorem calculator"; blog: synthetic vs long division — when each works.

### 5. Percentile & Percentile-Rank Calculator (method comparison) — 20/25
- **Category/slug:** /math/percentile-calculator/ (percentile-from-dataset + percentile rank of a value + class rank; distinct query family from the live statistics calculator)
- **Scores:** demand 4 · feasibility 5 · gap 3 · durability 3 · fit 5
- **Future driver:** the multiple-definitions confusion is structural — Excel's PERCENTILE.INC vs .EXC vs nearest-rank give different answers and incumbents silently pick one; a method-comparison tool answers the recurring "why does my answer differ from Excel/my textbook" pain. Stats coursework + admissions (class rank) perennial.
- **Evidence:** omni percentile-rank, calculatorsoup, emathhelp percentile-class-rank, dedicated domain classrankcalculator.xyz (demand proof), calculator-online.net. URLs: https://www.omnicalculator.com/statistics/percentile-rank · https://www.calculatorsoup.com/calculators/statistics/percentile-calculator.php · https://www.emathhelp.net/calculators/probability-statistics/percentile-class-rank-calculator/ · https://classrankcalculator.xyz/
- **Gap:** incumbents compute one method without naming it; showing nearest-rank, linear-interpolation (INC/R-7) and EXC side by side with the position arithmetic shown is genuinely rare. Overlap check: live statistics calculator shows quartiles only (Moore & McCabe, named); `src/lib/mathx.ts` already contains `percentileValue` (R-7) and `percentileRank` — the engine half-exists, the page and the method-comparison do not. Distinct intent; cross-link, don't merge.
- **Feasibility:** pure JS on sorted arrays; add EXC + nearest-rank variants to mathx.
- **MVP:** dataset + p → percentile value by three named methods with position math shown; value → percentile rank; rank + class size → top-X% (class rank mode).
- **SEO angle:** "percentile calculator", "percentile rank calculator", "why is my percentile different in excel", "class rank percentile"; blog: the percentile-definition mess, explained.

## Watchlist

- **Weighted average / grade calculator** — 19/25 (demand 5 · feas 5 · **gap 2** · durability 3 · fit 4) — blocked by gap. Highest raw demand this round: calculator.net, calculatorsoup, rapidtables, omni, inchcalculator ("with steps") plus multiple dedicated domains (gradecalculator.mes.fm, gradecalculatortools.com) all free and adequate; the killer sub-intent ("what do I need on my final") is already owned by calculator.net. Exactness barely matters for grades. Promote only with proof of an underserved sub-intent (e.g., fraction/credit-hour weights with exact steps, or a "weighted average" non-grade intent like GPA-by-credits gaps).
- **Absolute value / inequality solver** — 17/25 (demand 4 · feas 4 · **gap 2** · durability 3 · fit 4). The constrained |ax+b| ⋚ c form we could do exactly is precisely what free incumbents already serve with steps + interval notation + number line (Symbolab free tier, calcforlife, easymathtools, quickmath). Promote if a gap appears on exact-fraction endpoints or compound inequalities.
- **Exponent rules / laws-of-exponents simplifier** — 16/25 (demand 3 · feas 4 · **gap 2** · durability 3 · fit 4). Requires an expression parser (scope creep vs our form-input tools); Symbolab/Mathway/SnapXam/Pearson/8gwifi all free with steps. Promote only with a constrained high-volume sub-intent (e.g., "negative exponent calculator" long-tail gap evidence).
- **Prior watchlist (systems 18, matrix 17, sequences 17, π-geometry 16, probability 15, logarithm 15, base-N 14):** re-examined per brief — no new keyword-gap evidence surfaced in this round's searches; no score changes, no promotions. Noted: Pearson's calculator channel keeps expanding (seen in radians + exponent queries), which *worsens* the gap axis for steps-based head terms over time — argues for building our gap-3 items sooner rather than later.

# Next-Vein Scan — Strongest Category After Statistics + Color — 2026-07-11

Owner-directed "what do we build next" scan, run in the same posture as the recent `/calc/`, `/biology/`, `/generate/`, `/web3/`, `/color/` expansions: a **ranked build list**, not a gap-gated go/no-go. Scoring uses the house x/25 rubric (**demand · competition-gap · durability · on-brand-differentiation · build-cost**, where build-cost 5 = cheapest). `[S]` = declarative fields→compute island; `[C]` = custom island (parser / matrix / interactive). `[D!]` = data-staleness risk, `[D!-lite]` = trivially-pinnable.

## Executive summary

The single strongest next vein is a **new `/chemistry/` category (Chemistry & Lab Calculators)** — the missing coherent sibling of the education cluster that has been LazyTools' most productive vein (`/math/` → `/statistics/` → `/biology/`). It was already explicitly teed up as a future anchor in the 2026-07-10 biology scan (Henderson-Hasselbalch, molarity and Beer-Lambert were *deferred to a future Chemistry category*). It clears the current filters better than another consumer-calc or generator tranche because its core tools are **deterministic and byte-exact** (molar mass from fixed atomic-weight constants; equation balancing is exact integer linear algebra), **staleness-proof** (IUPAC standard atomic weights change on the order of once every two decades per element — the Jan-2025 revision touched only Gd/Lu/Zr, irrelevant to common compounds), and **strongly AI-resistant** (the published Journal of Chemical Education literature and multiple documented cases show ChatGPT/GPT-4 mis-balancing equations, mis-identifying the limiting reagent, and mis-applying stoichiometric coefficients — exactly the class of task a chatbot can't be trusted with).

As with every education category, competition-gap is a **structural 2–3** — WebQC, Pearson, Omni, PlanetCalc, calculator-online.net and a wave of "free stoichiometry calculator" clones already exist and are client-side. So "we don't upload" wins nothing; the edge is the house thesis: **exact working shown (integer-coefficient balancing via nullspace, hydrate/nested-parenthesis-correct molar mass parsing), clean single-purpose ad-free SEO pages, honest sourcing (pinned CIAAW atomic-weight table version), and cross-linking into the existing `/biology/`, `/math/` and `/statistics/` clusters.** Molarity, dilution (C1V1=C2V2), and Beer-Lambert re-home cleanly from `/biology/`, so the category also tidies a known boundary.

**Recommendation: GO (qualified).** Build the **molar-mass formula parser first** (its element-table + tokenizer core is shared by stoichiometry, empirical formula and percent composition), then the **equation balancer** (exact rational linear-algebra core — the flagship AI-resistant page), then **stoichiometry** (reuses both), then the cheap `[S]` formula tools.

Runner-up veins considered and parked: a `/dev/` data-format expansion (JSON/JWT/regex/cron/timestamp — durable and AI-resistant, but the most commoditized niche on the web and largely already covered by our `/dev/` + `/network/` tools) and a broad Physics category (too fragmented; its highest-demand pieces — ideal gas, specific heat — are absorbed into Chemistry anyway). Neither beats Chemistry on coherence.

## Why Chemistry clears the filters

- **Deterministic / byte-exact:** molar mass is a summation over a fixed constant table; percent composition and empirical formula follow exactly; **equation balancing is a homogeneous linear system solved for the smallest positive integer nullspace vector** (Gaussian elimination over rationals / BigInt) — one correct answer, shown with working.
- **AI-resistance (≥3/5):** deterministic exact output ✔; zero-friction beats prompting (type a formula, get mass + breakdown) ✔; repeat-workflow (students use weekly through a term, bookmarked) ✔; plus documented chatbot unreliability on this exact task class. Not privacy-sensitive and not binary-file, but 3/5 is met.
- **Named driver:** evergreen chemistry education (secondary + intro undergrad + lab bench) — the same durable, non-API driver `/math/`, `/calc/` and `/biology/` were accepted under. Not strengthening via a new web API, but structurally permanent and staleness-proof, which is the point.
- **Not already built:** no `/chemistry/` category exists; only `molarity` and `dilution` sit (arguably mis-homed) in `/biology/` — this scan re-homes them.

## Ranked build list

### LEAD

**1. Molar mass / molecular weight calculator — 19/25 `[C]`**
- **Slugs:** `/chemistry/molar-mass-calculator/` (flagship) + SEO satellites `/chemistry/molecular-weight-calculator/`, `/chemistry/percent-composition-calculator/` (percent composition is a *view* of the same parse — one island, distinct pages).
- **Scores:** demand 5 · gap 2 · durability 5 · differentiation 4 · build-cost 3.
- **Driver:** evergreen chem education (every intro course); "molar mass of …" is a permanent high-volume long-tail.
- **Algorithm (deterministic):** tokenize formula → recursive-descent parse of nested parentheses/brackets, element symbols (two-letter greedy: `Co` vs `C`+`o`), subscripts, hydrate dot `·`/`.`/`*` (e.g. `CuSO4·5H2O`), and optional charge; sum `Σ count × standardAtomicWeight`. Percent composition = each element's mass contribution ÷ total. Atomic weights from a **pinned CIAAW abridged standard-atomic-weights table** (frozen constant array in `src/data/chemistry/atomic-weights.ts`).
- **Differentiation:** the AI-resistant wedge is **correct parsing of hard formulas** (hydrates, nested groups like `Ca(NO3)2`, `Fe2(SO4)3`, `KAl(SO4)2·12H2O`) plus a per-element mass/percent breakdown table and honest sourcing note. Chatbots and thin clones fumble hydrates and two-letter-symbol ambiguity.
- **[D!-lite]:** pin the CIAAW table version on-page; add a one-line note that the Jan-2025 Gd/Lu/Zr revision is negligible for common compounds. Zero ongoing maintenance in practice.

**2. Chemical equation balancer — 20/25 `[C]` — the flagship AI-resistant page**
- **Slug:** `/chemistry/equation-balancer/` (satellite `/chemistry/balancing-equations-calculator/`).
- **Scores:** demand 5 · gap 2 · durability 5 · differentiation 5 · build-cost 3.
- **Driver:** evergreen; "balance chemical equation" is one of the highest-volume chemistry queries (WebQC/Pearson/PlanetCalc all monetize it).
- **Algorithm (exact):** parse each species into an element-count vector (reuse the molar-mass parser); build the element × species stoichiometric matrix with products negated; solve the homogeneous system `A·x = 0` by **exact rational Gaussian elimination** (BigInt fractions, no float) for the 1-D nullspace; scale to **smallest positive integers via LCM of denominators ÷ GCD**. Detect and report rank deficiency > 1 (under-determined / needs additional constraint) and impossible balances. Optional **charge row** for ionic/net-ionic equations.
- **Differentiation (5):** **byte-exact integer coefficients with the linear-algebra working shown** — this is precisely the task the published record shows LLMs failing (mis-applied coefficients, matrix-method errors after the row-echelon step). We show the matrix + nullspace + integer scaling. Incumbents give the answer; few show exact rational working, and none lead with the "why a chatbot gets this wrong" explainer. **Build the exact-rational core here — it is reused by stoichiometry and empirical formula.**

**3. Stoichiometry calculator (mole ↔ mass ↔ particles, limiting reagent, theoretical + percent yield) — 18/25 `[C]`**
- **Slug:** `/chemistry/stoichiometry-calculator/`.
- **Scores:** demand 5 · gap 2 · durability 5 · differentiation 4 · build-cost 2.
- **Driver:** evergreen; core of every intro-chem course and exam.
- **Algorithm:** reuse balancer + molar-mass parser; given a balanced (or auto-balanced) equation and one known amount, convert grams↔moles↔particles (Avogadro constant, fixed), apply molar ratios, identify the **limiting reagent** (min mole-ratio), compute theoretical yield, and percent yield from a user-supplied actual yield. All rational/step-shown.
- **Differentiation:** limiting-reagent + theoretical-yield with per-step working is exactly the documented ChatGPT failure mode (wrong limiting reactant). Deterministic, repeat-workflow, exam-driven. Build-cost 2 because it composes two custom cores and needs careful UX, so ship after #1 and #2.

### SOLID (`[S]` unless noted — cheap, formula-exact, re-home the biology strays here)

**4. Molarity calculator (mass ↔ molarity ↔ volume) — 19/25 `[S]` — RE-HOME from `/biology/`**

- **Scores:** demand 4 · gap 2 · durability 5 · differentiation 3 · build-cost 5.

**5. Dilution calculator C1V1 = C2V2 (+ serial dilution) — 19/25 `[C-lite]` — SHARE with `/biology/`**
- **Slug:** `/chemistry/dilution-calculator/`. Already a `/biology/` LEAD; expose the same island under `/chemistry/` with a chemistry framing (stock→working solution) — one component, two category landing pages, cross-linked.
- **Scores:** demand 4 · gap 2 · durability 5 · differentiation 3 · build-cost 5.

**6. Ideal gas law PV = nRT solver — 19/25 `[S]`**
- **Slug:** `/chemistry/ideal-gas-law-calculator/`.
- **Scores:** demand 4 · gap 2 · durability 5 · differentiation 3 · build-cost 5.
- **Algorithm:** solve for any of P/V/n/T; R as a **fixed constant** with unit-consistent variants (0.082057 L·atm·mol⁻¹·K⁻¹, 8.314 J·mol⁻¹·K⁻¹, 62.36 L·Torr·mol⁻¹·K⁻¹); unit-aware inputs. FOLD combined gas law (P₁V₁/T₁ = P₂V₂/T₂) and Boyle/Charles/Gay-Lussac as modes.

**7. Specific heat / calorimetry q = mcΔT — 19/25 `[S]`**
- **Slug:** `/chemistry/specific-heat-calculator/`.
- **Scores:** demand 4 · gap 2 · durability 5 · differentiation 3 · build-cost 5.
- **Algorithm:** solve for any of q/m/c/ΔT; user-supplied specific heat (optional short reference list of common substances as a **stable constant table**, user-overridable). High cross-over chem/physics demand.

**8. pH / pOH / [H⁺] / [OH⁻] calculator — 18/25 `[S]`**
- **Slug:** `/chemistry/ph-calculator/`.
- **Scores:** demand 4 · gap 2 · durability 5 · differentiation 3 · build-cost 4.
- **Algorithm:** `pH = -log₁₀[H⁺]`, `pOH = -log₁₀[OH⁻]`, `pH + pOH = 14` (Kw at 25 °C, stated), interconvert all four. FOLD a strong-acid/strong-base concentration→pH mode.

**9. Empirical & molecular formula from percent composition — 18/25 `[C]`**
- **Slug:** `/chemistry/empirical-formula-calculator/`.
- **Scores:** demand 4 · gap 2 · durability 5 · differentiation 4 · build-cost 3.
- **Algorithm:** percent (or mass) → divide by atomic weight → divide by smallest → **rationalize to smallest whole-number ratio** (round-to-integer with tolerance, or continued-fraction to catch ×1.5/×1.33 cases → multiply); molecular formula from empirical mass vs given molar mass (reuse the molar-mass parser). Deterministic, exam-driven, AI-resistant (the rationalization step is where chatbots slip).

## DO-NOT-BUILD (with reasons)

- **Interactive periodic table as an SEO bet** — ptable.com utterly owns it; it is reference data, not calculation. Build a *lightweight element-data reference* only as internal support for molar mass, not a traffic page.
- **Reaction / product predictor ("what does A + B make")** — requires chemical knowledge and heuristics, not deterministic math; this is AI territory and would rot / mislead. Excluded.
- **Named-reaction / mechanism / concept explainers** — AI-writing exclusion.
- **Solubility-rules / activity-series / reactivity lookup tables** — curated reference data with a maintenance/authority surface; staleness filter (same reason pet-drug and species tables were rejected in `/biology/`). `[D!]`
- **Nomenclature (IUPAC name ↔ formula) generator** — huge rules surface, edge-case-ridden, and chatbots do the fuzzy version acceptably; weak exactness wedge. Watchlist at best, not a launch tool.
- **Lewis-structure / VSEPR geometry drawer** — graphical, hard, heuristic edge cases; off-brand and not cleanly deterministic. Reject/deep-watchlist.
- **Electron-configuration generator** — deterministic (Aufbau + known exceptions) but ptable/many tools own it and the exception list is a small `[D!-lite]` curation surface; watchlist only with long-tail proof.
- **Titration curve / full equilibrium ICE-table solver with Ka/Kb tables** — the Ka/Kb reference tables are a `[D!]` curated-data liability; a *user-supplied-Ka* ICE calculator is a possible future `[C]` but not a launch tool.
- **Thermodynamics with ΔHf°/S°/ΔGf° standard-enthalpy tables** — `[D!]` curated reference tables; excluded on the staleness filter. (A user-supplies-values Hess's-law adder could be a thin `[S]` later.)

## Data-staleness flags

- **Atomic-weight table `[D!-lite]`** — the one shared dependency. Pin a CIAAW abridged standard-atomic-weights version in `src/data/chemistry/atomic-weights.ts`, cite it on every molar-mass-derived page, and note the Jan-2025 Gd/Lu/Zr revision as negligible for common compounds. Revisions are ~once-per-two-decades per element, so this is a one-time pin, not the LLM-pricing-class recurring ritual. Everything else in the category is a fixed physical constant (R, Avogadro, Kw at 25 °C — all stated with their temperature/basis) or user-supplied → staleness-proof.

## Build architecture / order

Shared cores, built once, reused across the cluster (this is why Chemistry is cheap after the first two tools):
1. **`atomic-weights.ts`** (pinned constant table) + **formula parser/tokenizer** (nested parens, hydrates, two-letter greedy symbols, charges) — powers molar mass, percent composition, stoichiometry, empirical formula.
2. **Exact rational linear-algebra core** (BigInt fraction Gaussian elimination → integer nullspace) — powers the equation balancer and the balancing step inside stoichiometry.
3. `[S]` field→compute islands for molarity, dilution, ideal gas, specific heat, pH, Henderson-Hasselbalch, Beer-Lambert — trivial once the pattern from `/calc/`/`/biology/` is reused.

**Order:** molar mass `[C]` → equation balancer `[C]` (exact core) → stoichiometry `[C]` → the `[S]` tranche (molarity/dilution re-home first, then ideal gas / specific heat / pH) → empirical formula `[C]` → Henderson-Hasselbalch / Beer-Lambert landings.
